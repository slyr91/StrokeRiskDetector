Identifying Risks

Machine learning is a powerful tool that is revolutionizing the world. The machine learning algorithm used by this site is known as a Logistic Regression Classifier and it is very good at detecting patterns. It can then identify a classification that those patterns correlate to.

Correlations can help us as humans identify some of the patterns that this algorithm will take advantage of to classify whether or not a patient is likely to have a stroke in the future. Hopefully this information will aid you in making decisions that benefit the patients that are within your care.

Below is a heatmap of all the factors that the algorithm is looking at. As you can see the largest correlation by far in the stroke cross section is age.

Age is a big factor and it has definitely influenced the algorithm during the learning phase of development. As evidenced in the following graph, the stroke risk estimator has calculated the older patients with a higher risk value.

While it may appear that age is a huge risk factor it is not the only risk. In fact, age is such a high risk factor because of the fact that other high risk factors are more likely to develop the older a person gets. Hypertension and heart disease are major contributors to having a stroke and you are more likely to develop these conditions as you get older.

The following graph shows the number of people, depicted as a percentage of the population, who have one or both conditions.

All these risk factors, and more, help contribute to the likelihood of a patient having a stroke. It is much harder for a human, even an expertly trained human, to see all the factors and identify a person as being high risk for having a stroke. By using machine learning we can help doctors identify high risk patients and automate patient care response.
